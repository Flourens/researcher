Organization: HK-dir (Directorate for Higher Education and Skills, Norway)
Programme: Nansen Support Programme for Ukraine
Funder: Norwegian Ministry of Foreign Affairs
Deadline: Extended (original: 2 March 2026, 13:00 CET)
Budget: Up to NOK 80,000,000 total; up to NOK 5,000,000 per project (~EUR 430,000)
Duration: Up to 3 years (start: June-July 2026; end: no later than June 30, 2029)

Application Language: English
Contact: ukraina@hkdir.no

=== PROGRAMME OBJECTIVES ===

The Nansen EDU programme aims to:
1. Strengthen Ukrainian institutions' capacity to deliver quality education
2. Improve access to qualified professionals in priority fields
3. Establish long-term cooperation between Norwegian and Ukrainian higher education partners

=== THEMATIC PRIORITIES ===

Projects must focus on one or more of the following thematic areas:
- Digital transformation
- Environment and energy
- Physical and mental health
- Law, social sciences, economics
- Reconstruction and urban planning
- STEM and agricultural sciences
- Teacher education

=== ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA ===

Applicant Requirements:
- Only Norwegian higher education institutions accredited by NOKUT can apply
- Must offer bachelor's and master's programs
- Must coordinate projects involving at least two Ukrainian partner institutions
- Ukrainian partners must be fully recognized, offering both bachelor's and master's level programs
- Encouraged to include institutions relocated or affected by the war in Ukraine
- European partners may participate supplementarily if contributing meaningfully

Consortium Composition:
- Applicant/Coordinator: 1 Norwegian HEI (mandatory)
- Ukrainian partners: Minimum 2 Ukrainian HEIs (mandatory)
- Additional European partners: Optional (if meaningful contribution)

=== WHAT CAN THE FUNDS BE USED FOR ===

Eligible cost categories:
1. Personnel and administration costs (account category 5***):
   - Salaries for project participants (release time or new hires)
   - IMPORTANT: Maximum 30% of total budget for Norwegian/non-Ukrainian partner personnel
   - Hourly rates must include all non-wage costs (holiday pay, insurance, indirect costs)

2. Procurement costs for services (account category 6***):
   - External services necessary for project implementation
   - Must be clearly justified as necessary

3. Procurement costs for equipment (account category 4***):
   - Physical items, licenses, software necessary for the project
   - Must be purchased no later than 12 months before project end

4. Scholarship costs (account category 5***):
   - Scholarships for Ukrainian students (up to 10 months)
   - Bachelor's/Master's: EUR 250/month maximum
   - PhD: EUR 400/month maximum
   - Must be jointly awarded by Norwegian and Ukrainian partners

5. Travel and accommodation costs (account category 7***):
   - Project participant travel (domestic and international)
   - Student exchange travel costs
   - Must follow Norwegian State Personnel Handbook rates
   - Digital alternatives encouraged; climate-friendly transport preferred

6. Scholarships for mobility:
   - Student exchanges: up to 6 months per person
   - Staff exchanges: up to 30 days per stay (one or more stays)
   - Incoming students to Norway (>4 weeks): Norwegian State Educational Loan Fund rates
   - Incoming students (<4 weeks): government subsistence rates

7. Other project-related costs (account categories 6 and 7***):
   - Costs not fitting other categories but necessary for implementation
   - Must be justified

=== CROSS-CUTTING ISSUES (MANDATORY) ===

All projects must address:
1. Anti-corruption measures: Required for partners from countries with Corruption Perception Index ≤ 69 (Ukraine qualifies). Must implement preventive measures.
2. Climate and environment: Projects should reduce carbon footprint (digital meetings, climate-friendly travel)
3. Gender equality: Gender dimension must be addressed in project design and implementation
4. Human rights: Must assess potential negative impacts on participation, accountability, non-discrimination, women's rights
5. SEAH prevention: Must have ethical guidelines for prevention and follow-up of sexual exploitation, abuse, and harassment

=== RISK ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENTS ===

Projects must include risk assessments covering:
- Security situation in Ukraine (especially for institutions in conflict-affected areas)
- Corruption risk (Ukraine CPI < 69)
- Environmental and climate risks
- Social discrimination risks
- Data protection and IT security
- Academic freedom and national security interests
- Export control and dual use considerations

=== EVALUATION CRITERIA ===

Applications are assessed on 4 criteria with the following weights:

Criterion 1: Relevance of the project (30%)
Sub-factors:
1.1) Need for the project and significance for target groups
1.2) Planned results are concrete and suitable to achieve call objectives
1.3) Level of ambition of results
1.4) Innovation and/or complementarity to other measures/activities
1.5) Knowledge of existing initiatives; added value beyond what exists

Criterion 2: Quality of project design and implementation (20%)
Sub-factors:
2.1) Concrete, comprehensive and realistic implementation plan
2.2) Quality of risk assessments, including cross-cutting issues and SEAH
2.3) Cost-effectiveness and sufficient resource allocation

Criterion 3: Quality of the project group and collaborative structures (20%)
Sub-factors:
3.1) Complementarity of institutions/actors and overall expertise
3.2) Division of tasks shows commitment and active contribution from all partners
3.3) Clear and comprehensive plan for interaction between partners and stakeholders

Criterion 4: Project impact (30%)
Sub-factors:
4.1) Potential impact for target groups, institutions, and actors in and outside the project
4.2) Quality of measures to ensure impacts are maintained after project end
4.3) Quality of measures to evaluate and document project impact

=== GRADING SCALE ===

7 - Exceptional: Meets assessment factors in exceptional manner, no or only minor potential for improvement
6 - Excellent: Meets factors in excellent manner, very few or only minor potential for improvement
5 - Very good: Meets factors in very good manner, some clear potential for improvement, no significant shortcomings
4 - Good: Meets factors in good manner, some clear potential for improvement or significant shortcomings
3 - Fair: Meets factors in fair manner, substantial potential for improvement or significant shortcomings
2 - Poor: Meets factors in inadequate manner, decisive potential for improvement and/or shortcomings
1 - Very poor: Meets factors in clearly inadequate manner, very poorly described and justified

QUALIFICATION THRESHOLD: All sub-grades must be ≥ 4; at least two sub-grades must be ≥ 5

=== APPLICATION DOCUMENTS REQUIRED ===

1. Project Description (PDF, using HK-dir template) — uploaded to Espresso
2. Signed Declaration of Participation from applicant's legal representative
3. Letters of Commitment from all partners (excluding applicant)
4. Project Coordinator CV (maximum 4 pages)
5. Budget (completed in Espresso portal)

=== PROJECT DESCRIPTION TEMPLATE STRUCTURE ===

Section 1: Relevance of the project
  1.1 Background and needs of the project
  1.2 The project's alignment with other activities/measures and any innovative elements

Section 2: Design and implementation
  2.1 Activities, measures and approaches to achieve the project results
  2.2 Risk factors in the project and measures to reduce them

Section 3: The project group and collaborative structures
  3.1 Key personnel for the implementation of the project
  3.2 Involvement, interaction and division of work between the project partners

Section 4: Project impact
  4.1 The project impact and possible measures to promote dissemination
  4.2 Duration of the impact and evaluation of the project's impact

Section 5: Reference list
