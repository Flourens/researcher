FFplus Call-2-Type-2: Evaluation Criteria & Proposal Requirements
Source: Proposers Guide (Version 21/11/2025)
Extracted for use by AI reviewer agent.

Call: Second call for innovation studies for the development
      of generative AI models
Deadline: Feb 25, 2026 17:00 Brussels time (or when 250 proposals received)
Duration: 10 months (targeted start September 1, 2026)
Total budget: EUR 4M indicative

======================================================================
1. EVALUATION CRITERIA
======================================================================

--- 1.1 Impact ---
- Innovation prospects of the proposed study
- Commercial viability
- Societal relevance (where applicable)
- Vision of success and value creation/proposition
- Alignment with the SME's business models and exploitation plans
- Alignment with the objectives of the call (generative AI + HPC)

--- 1.2 Excellence ---
  Covers BOTH conceptual and technical excellence:

  (a) Conceptual Excellence:
  - Conceptual soundness
  - Cohesiveness of the proposal
  - Articulation of plans for bridging gaps to ensure successful
      implementation and impact

  (b) Technical Excellence:
  - Clear definition of technical requirements
  - Justification of technology choices
  - Articulation of performance metrics for model evaluation,
      scaling, and optimization
  - Activities for establishing baseline performance
  - Ensuring experiment reproducibility

--- 1.3 Implementation ---
- Quality of the project's workplan
- Quality of the data management plan
- Distribution of resources to additional organisations (where applicable)
- Capacity of the applicant(s) to carry out the proposed work
- Justification for computation resources required

======================================================================
2. SCORING SYSTEM
======================================================================
- Each criterion is scored on a scale of 0 to 5.
- All three criteria are EQUALLY WEIGHTED.
- Maximum possible total score: 15 (3 criteria x 5 points each).

======================================================================
3. THRESHOLDS
======================================================================
- Overall acceptance threshold: 10 (sum of all three criteria scores).
- Minimum per-criterion threshold: 3 (each criterion must score >= 3).
- A proposal scoring below 3 on ANY single criterion is rejected,
    even if the total exceeds 10.

======================================================================
4. TIE-BREAKING RULES
======================================================================
  When proposals have the same overall score, they are ranked by
  individual criterion scores in the following priority order:

- Priority 1: Impact score (higher is better)
- Priority 2: Excellence score (higher is better)
- Priority 3: Implementation score (higher is better)
- Priority 4: Total requested funding (lower is better)

======================================================================
5. BUDGET REQUIREMENTS AND LIMITS
======================================================================

--- 5.1 Overall Funding ---
- Total indicative budget for all sub-projects: EUR 4 million
- 100% funding of incurred, eligible DIRECT costs
- No indirect costs or overheads are funded
- Sub-contracting costs are NOT eligible

--- 5.2 Per-Proposal Limits ---
- Maximum funding for main participant (SME): EUR 200,000
- Maximum total funding for multi-partner consortium: EUR 300,000
- Maximum total funding for single-partner (no supporting partners): EUR 200,000
- At least 50% of requested funding must be allocated to the main participant
    (deviations must be duly justified)

--- 5.3 Supporting Participant Limits ---
- Maximum EUR 150,000 per supporting participant under this call
- Maximum EUR 300,000 cumulative across ALL FFplus innovation studies
- Only engineering/technical activities are eligible for supporting participants
- Business consultancy, marketing, admin tasks are NOT eligible

--- 5.4 Eligible Budget Modules ---
- Personnel: costs commensurate with work to be performed
- Equipment: depreciation costs only; specialized equipment only
    (no laptops, monitors, common-use equipment)
- Travel: must be justified as necessary for the workplan
- HPC compute capacity: must be justified; EuroHPC systems preferred
    (decision not to use EuroHPC must be justified)
- Material: specialized SW licenses; data sets/collections
    (general office software licenses not eligible)

--- 5.5 HPC Resources ---
- FFplus does NOT provide HPC resources itself
- Sub-projects expected to use EuroHPC JU access schemes (free of charge)
- AI Factories access scheme is the recommended option
- Requirements should clearly exceed 'playground access' allocations
- 3rd-party commercial HPC allowed if justified; costs in budget
- HPC from supporting partner must be at actual cost, not commercial rates

======================================================================
6. PAGE LIMITS AND FORMATTING RULES
======================================================================

--- 6.1 Proposal Structure ---
- Proposal has two parts: Part A (spreadsheet) and Part B (PDF)
- Part B PDF must not exceed 5.0 MB
- Submission language: English

--- 6.2 Part B Page Limit ---
- Maximum 13 pages (including references and appendices)
- The cover page is EXCLUDED from the 13-page count
- EXCEEDING 13 PAGES => IMMEDIATE REJECTION without evaluation

--- 6.3 Font and Formatting ---
- Minimum font size: 11-point Arial
- This applies also to images containing textual information

--- 6.4 Part B Recommended Structure (with indicative lengths) ---
- Section 1: Summary (0.5 pages)
- Section 2: Industrial relevance, potential impact (incl. societal),
    exploitation plans (3.5 pages)
- Section 3: Work plan, technological/algorithmic approach,
    software development strategy (5 pages)
- Section 4: Quality of the consortium and individual proposers (1-2 pages)
- Section 5: Justification of costs and resources (1-2 pages)

--- 6.5 Part A Requirements ---
- Use the provided template spreadsheet exactly
- Do NOT delete or rename worksheets, rows, or columns
- Include only requested information; extraneous data will be deleted
- Major deviations from the template => rejection without evaluation

--- 6.6 Consistency Requirement ---
- Part A and Part B must provide CONSISTENT information
- Inconsistent information (e.g., budget figures) => rejection without evaluation

======================================================================
7. ELIGIBILITY AND CONSORTIUM RULES (ADMINISTRATIVE CHECK)
======================================================================
- Main participant must be an SME or Start-up
- Maximum 2 supporting participants (total consortium <= 3 partners)
- Each partner needs a clearly defined role
- Organisations must be established in an EU Member State, or listed
    EEA / Digital Europe Programme associated countries
- Natural persons (individuals) are NOT eligible
- FFplus beneficiaries are ineligible as main or supporting participants
- Participation in Type-1 (business experiments) and Type-2 (innovation
    studies) is mutually exclusive for main SME participants
- Must document SME status and self-assessment of EU trustworthy AI guidelines

  NOTE: Proposals violating items 1-8 of the eligibility criteria are rejected
  WITHOUT technical evaluation.

======================================================================
8. EVALUATION PROCEDURE
======================================================================

--- 8.1 Steps ---
- Step 1: Proposal submission
- Step 2: Admissibility/eligibility check
- Step 3: First-stage evaluation (initial ranking) -- if > 100 eligible proposals
- Step 4: Final selection (second-stage evaluation, consensus review)
- Step 5: Funding decision (final, no appeals)

--- 8.2 First-Stage Evaluation (if applicable) ---
- Applied when admissible/eligible proposals exceed 100
- Individual assessments by 2 independent external experts per proposal
- Top proposals up to 3x the fundable number advance to second stage
- Rejected proposers receive a short evaluation summary report

--- 8.3 Second-Stage / Final Evaluation ---
- Consensus review process between the 2 expert evaluators
- Moderator facilitates consensus assessment report
- Consensus report becomes the evaluation summary report shared with proposers

--- 8.4 Evaluators ---
- Two independent external experts per proposal
- Experts have demonstrated competency in the proposal's field
- Assessments are done independently of one another

======================================================================
9. KEY EXPECTATIONS FOR PROPOSALS (REVIEWER CHECKLIST)
======================================================================
  The proposal should demonstrate ALL of the following:

- Full alignment with FFplus call objectives (generative AI + HPC for SMEs)
- Justification for why generative AI is needed and why a new model is
    imperative (not solvable by few-shot prompting, existing models, etc.)
- Vision of success: how large-scale HPC leads to positive business impact
- Specific objectives with action plan covering the ML lifecycle:
    data preparation, model development/engineering, model evaluation
- Detailed training dataset description and demonstrated availability
- Model characteristics: type, size, hyperparameters, architecture
- Performance metrics and reproducibility plan with baselines/benchmarks
- Risk identification per EU trustworthy AI guidelines
    (unfairness, bias, hallucinations, model drift)
- Justified resource allocation (personnel, IT/computing, other)
- Comprehensive data management plan (FAIR principles)
- Proficiency in generative AI and HPC MUST be documented
    (failure = misalignment with FFplus objectives)

  NOTE: Model exploitation, deployment, or operation are OUT OF SCOPE.
  NOTE: Revenue generated during funding phase must be reported and
  impacts final funding.

======================================================================
10. MONTH 7 REPORT REQUIREMENT
======================================================================
- All innovation studies must deliver an intermediate report by end of Month 7
- Reports cover results achieved and potential impact
- Evaluated by 2 independent external experts + consensus meeting
- Top 70% of innovation studies (by ranking) become eligible to submit
    extension proposals in the subsequent open call
