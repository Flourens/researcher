ABOUT COP-PILOT:
COP-PILOT is an Innovation Action funded under the EU HORIZON program focused on developing a Collaborative Open Platform (COP) framework that enables end-to-end orchestration of services across diverse industry sectors. Building upon the foundations of edge computing, advanced 5G connectivity, and decentralized processing, COP-PILOT aims to overcome fragmented infrastructure and isolated service domains by providing a unified platform for secure, automated, and intelligent management of resources and data.

At its core, COP-PILOT integrates novel AI-driven tools and automation solutions with robust security features to facilitate the onboarding and management of complex applications. The project prioritizes modularity, ease-of-use, and industry-standard interoperability, leveraging multi-tiered data processing, policy-driven optimization, and dynamic reasoning for service orchestration. Through hierarchical orchestration and resource slicing, COP-PILOT enables scalable, reliable, and SLA-preserving deployment of vertical services.

=== CL2 CLUSTER SPECIFICS — Smart Sustainable IoT Solutions (Valencia) ===

CLUSTER OVERVIEW:
Valencia's piloting cluster focuses on smart sustainable IoT solutions. The cluster brings several real-life environments for use case demonstration: (i) the city of Valencia, (ii) the port of Valencia — the fifth-largest port in Europe, (iii) the nearby industrial park of Almussafes, and (iv) UPV campus, a realistic smart city environment aiming carbon neutral by 2030. The cluster implements smart IoT platforms including the Thinking City platform by Telefonica (based on FIWARE, used by City of Valencia and UPV) and two platforms by IoT device integrators.

EXISTING USE CASES IN CL2:
- UC#2.1A: 5G-Connected Radars for Traffic Classification and Vehicle Counting — deployment of 5G-connected radar units for high-precision vehicle classification and real-time counting
- UC#2.1B: Flood Warning and Mitigation System Through Radar Sensing — connected radar units at vulnerable locations of Almussafes for real-time water level monitoring
- UC#2.2: Smart Resources Management in the UPV Campus — IoT-based fill-level sensors in waste containers for accurate waste generation estimation and distribution optimization
- UC#2.3: Maritime Traffic Monitoring and Berthing Assistance — radars at port for real-time vessel direction, position, and docking conditions monitoring
- UC#2.4A: IoT-Driven Smart Building Management — hybrid IoT architecture at La Harinera (multipurpose public building) integrating sensors with cloud-based BMS for occupancy, environmental conditions, space usage monitoring
- UC#2.4B: IoT-Based Environmental Quality Monitoring System — LoRaWAN sensor network in Juan Carlos I Industrial Park for real-time formaldehyde monitoring

OPEN CALL CHALLENGES FOR CL2 (what applicants should address):

OC#2.1: Smart Access and Resource Management at UPV Campus
- Access management: sensorizing and remotely controlling motorized gates and barriers in parking areas
- Water resource management: replacing traditional water meters with smart LoRaWAN-enabled ones for real-time consumption monitoring, leak detection, and data-driven maintenance
- Technologies: IoT sensors, actuators (gate controllers, barrier sensors, smart water meters), private LoRaWAN network
- Implementation: UPV Campus parking areas, building entrances, water network nodes

OC#2.2: Robotic Patrol and Surveillance at UPV Campus (and/or Valencia City)
- Campus security: robotic dogs patrol predefined routes, detect unusual activity, provide live video and sensor data
- Urban collaboration: with Valencia local police for public safety operations, mobile surveillance
- Technologies: autonomous quadruped robots with cameras, LiDAR, environmental sensors; private 5G network; edge/cloud systems for real-time analytics
- Test sites: UPV campus, selected urban streets in collaboration with Valencia local police

OC#2.3: Autonomous Terminal Shuttle (Port of Valencia)
- Deploying autonomous shuttle buses to transport employees and workers within the port
- Integrating private 5G networks, advanced network slicing, and V2X communication
- Technologies: 5G SA private network, V2I/V2V communication, remote-control cockpit
- Requires ultra-reliable, low-latency connectivity

OC#2.4: Municipal Buildings Management
- Install further IoT devices to measure, analyse, and visualize the behaviour of municipal buildings in Valencia
- Existing sensors: wireless IoT and wired sensing for temperature, humidity, CO2, occupancy, and energy consumption
- Need: intelligent analytics that can translate raw data into concrete recommendations for facility managers
- CRITICAL NEED: "What is still missing is the closed-loop automation layer that turns those insights into direct operational impact"
- Required capabilities of the automation layer:
  * Adjust climate control parameters (heating/cooling/ventilation) based on occupancy, comfort targets, outdoor conditions, and energy cost
  * Switch equipment on/off or move it into low-power modes when spaces are not in use
  * Regulate and schedule energy consumption to avoid peaks and support sustainability targets
  * Detect operational anomalies (e.g., abnormal consumption, HVAC malfunction, comfort drifts) and trigger corrective actions or maintenance alerts
- Users: campus security staff, maintenance workers, UPV as the LoRaWAN network manager, students and staff

THINKING CITIES PLATFORM (Telefonica):
- IoT management platform based on FIWARE, layered architecture per UNE 178104:2017
- Core modules: IoT Agents (NGSI-LD, MQTT, HTTP), Orion Context Broker (NGSI v2), Cygnus data adapter, STH (Short-Term Historic Storage, MongoDB), BDA (Analytical Database, MySQL/PostgreSQL), CEP (Complex Event Processing, Esper engine), Big Data Stack (HDFS, Spark, Hive), CKAN Open Data Portal
- Security: Keystone IDM, KeyPass (XACML), PEP proxies, OAuth 2.0
- Integration: IoT Agents accept UL2.0, JSON over HTTP/MQTT, transform to NGSI context updates; bidirectional communication
- Documentation: https://thinking-cities.readthedocs.io/en/latest/index.html, https://github.com/telefonicaid
